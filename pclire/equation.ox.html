 LiRE_Equation

Equation offers functionality for formatting linear equations suitable for output. Equations consist of left hand side and right hand side terms, each term consisting of a coefficient, a variable, and an optional value. Terms that include coefficients identically zero are removed, and term with unit coefficients are simplified.
Example:
To represent the equation \(z_t = c + z_{t-1} + u_t\), one may use: 
decl eqnZ = new LiRE_Equation();
decl c = ... ;
decl Phi = ... ;


eqnZ.AddTerm(LHS, "z[t]", "", <1>);
eqnZ.AddTerm(RHS, "", "c", c);
eqnZ.AddTerm(RHS, "z[t-1]", "Phi", Phi);
eqnZ.AddTerm(RHS, "u[t]", "", <1>);
eqnZ.Print();
